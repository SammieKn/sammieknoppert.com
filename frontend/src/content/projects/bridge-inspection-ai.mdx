## The Challenge

Inspection notes are often captured in hazardous environments—next to moving highways, in confined spaces, or near active machinery.

Having performed these inspections myself, I know the frustration and danger involved. When you are standing meters away from speeding traffic, every second you spend looking down at your phone to type descriptions is a second you aren't paying attention to your surroundings.

Because of this pressure, field notes are often unstructured, brief, or incomplete. This creates a secondary problem: once back in the safety of the office, inspectors spend hours manually reconstructing context and formatting reports.

**The goal:** Minimize screen time in the field (Safety) while maximizing report quality (Efficiency).

## Strategic Approach

## The Solution: Context-Aware AI

A generic "Fix my grammar" prompt isn't enough for technical engineering reports. To solve this, I architected a pipeline that utilizes **Few-Shot Prompting** to mimic the specific voice and standards of our senior engineers.

**How it works:**
1.  **Structured Input:** The inspector selects the specific "Installation Type" (e.g., Concrete Bridge) in the app and enters rough bullet points describing the damage.
2.  **Context Retrieval:** The API uses that Installation Type to query a database of historical "Gold Standard" reports for that specific asset class.
3.  **Generative Output:** We feed the LLM the inspector's rough notes *plus* the "Gold Standard" examples. This teaches the model exactly how the final report should look before it generates the text.

**The Stack:**
* **Backend:** FastAPI & Pydantic (for rigorous data validation)
* **AI:** OpenAI API (utilizing dynamic few-shot prompting)

* **50% Reduction** in reporting and formatting time.
* **Increased Safety:** Inspectors spend less time staring at screens in hazardous zones.
* **Higher Quality:** Seniors now spend their time reviewing technical accuracy rather than correcting grammar.
