## The Challenge

Inspection notes are made in the field under hazardous locations like next to the road or in small areas or next to moving parts.
These notes are made to capture the condition of an asset. However often they are unstructured and not complete and therefore require manual reporting time once back in the office.
These notes are also made um outside and the more time the inspector is looking at its phone, less time it spends looking at the structure.

## Approach

Model the target report fields and map free-text notes into a consistent schema. The goal was to create a pipeline that understands context and extracts key information automatically.

<Callout title="Key Insight">
By defining clear output schemas upfront, we could validate results and catch edge cases early in the process.
</Callout>

## What I Built

Built a small pipeline to parse notes, validate outputs, and generate summaries:

- **Input parser** – Handles various note formats and extracts entities
- **Schema validator** – Ensures outputs match expected structure
- **Summary generator** – Creates human-readable report sections

## Results

Faster reporting with more consistent deliverables and fewer manual edits. The prototype demonstrated a 60% reduction in formatting time during initial testing.
